About Me

Iâ€™m Christian Amoah, a researcher committed to addressing the unpredictability of Earth's climate systems and their profound impacts on lives and infrastructure. My work centers on developing proactive, data-driven tools to anticipate and mitigate critical climate risks, particularly urban floods and water resource challenges. My academic journey began with a Civil Engineering degree at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, where I explored the intersection of engineering, hydrology, and environmental data science. Early projects, such as streamflow modeling and GIS-based flood mapping for the Sisa Catchment, sparked my passion for integrating data science with traditional water management approaches. These experiences instilled in me a strong desire to advance research that blends computational tools with practical solutions to tackle complex climate issues.

My research interests are diverse but interconnected, focusing on hydrological modeling and climate risk, where I aim to improve flood resilience. I am also passionate about GeoAI and machine learning, applying these cutting-edge techniques to forecast urban floods and inform infrastructure planning. Currently, I am participating in the INUNDATA project on urban flash flood prediction, and also comprises building machine learning models to identify historical flood events using CHIRPS precipitation data and earth observation imagery. This work is helping to create a comprehensive flood record that can enhance future forecasting and mitigation strategies.

Beyond research, I find joy in music and web development. Playing the guitar and keyboard-piano allows me to explore diverse genres and express creativity, while my interest in web development enables me to design and build interactive, functional web applications. The driving force behind my work is a belief in the power of integrating innovative computational tools with collaborative, practical solutions to mitigate climate risks. My ultimate goal is to pioneer adaptive strategies that enhance flood resilience and foster sustainable development. Through my research and projects, I strive to bridge the gap between climate variability and decision-making, contributing to a safer and more sustainable future. Feel free to explore my repositories to learn more about the work I am passionate about!
