About Me

I’m Christian Amoah, a researcher dedicated to addressing climate variability and its impacts on water resources and infrastructure. My expertise lies in developing proactive, data-driven solutions to manage urban floods and hydrological challenges.

I hold a BSc in Civil Engineering from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), where I honed my skills in hydrology, environmental engineering, and GIS. Early on, I conducted research on streamflow modeling and flood mapping in the Sisa Catchment, which fueled my interest in blending data science with water management.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing the guitar and keyboard-piano, exploring diverse music genres. My passion for web development drives me to create interactive applications, merging creativity with technology.
